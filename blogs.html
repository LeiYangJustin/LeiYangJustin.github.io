13/07/2025 | Challenge-based learning at InnoWing

If you are new to the field of education or teaching, you might find the similar terms—problem-based, project-based, and challenge-based learning—confusing, just as I did. Recently, I found a very useful link that differentiates these terms. I summarize their differences as follows.

Problem-based learning aims to enhance students' self-learning, reasoning, and critical thinking abilities. Hence, it focuses on improving students' intellectual abilities, but the problems presented to students do not necessarily need to stem from real-world scenarios.

Project-based learning builds on this by requiring students to deliver a solution to the real-world problem. Through implementing the solution, students are expected to develop skills related to the problem. This highlights the difference in expected outcomes and the pedagogical approach from the problem-based learning.

Challenge-based learning presents students with real-world problems for which the solutions are unknown or unclear. The quest for solutions distinguishes this approach as a challenge rather than a project with predetermined answers. In this pedagogical model, students collaborate with teachers and/or experts to develop solutions develop solutions for real-world issues, fostering deeper knowledge and the creation of innovative tools.

In April 2025, InnoWing and HKUEAA launched three collaborative projects with industries in 3D data processing, AI-powered computer vision, autonomous drone technology, and robot design. Our goal is to provide students with challenge-based learning opportunities that enhance technical skills, cultivate teamwork, and inspire innovation. This experience will give students valuable insights into interdisciplinary collaboration, empowering them to become leaders in their fields.
