Research Interests

I am interested in geometry modeling and processing problems related to finite-element analysis and structural design optimization.
In my current study, I try to faciliate finite element modeling in structural optimization with mesh deformation methods.

My publications are listed below:

Lei Yang, Baojun Li, Zhangquan Lv, Wenbin Hou, Ping Hu,
Finite-element Mesh Deformation with the Skeleton-Section Template. To be appeared in Computer-Aided Design. doi:10.1016/j.cad.2015.11.002
To develop fast Finite Element (FE) adaptation methods for simulation-driven design optimization, we propose a Radial Basis Functions (RBF) method with a Skeleton-Section template to globally and locally deform FE meshes of thin-walled beam structures. The Skeleton-Section template is automatically formulated from the input mesh and serves as a hierarchical parameterization for the FE meshes. With this hierarchical parameterization, the global and the local geometries of a thin-walled beam can be taken care of in the same framework, which is of importance for designing the engineering component. The RBF method is employed to locally and globally deform the mesh model with the cross-sections and the skeleton, respectively. The RBF method solves the spatial deformation field given prescribed deformations at the cross-sections. At the local scale, the user modifies the cross-sections to deform a region of the surface mesh. At the global level, the skeleton is manipulated and its deformation is transferred to all cross-sections to induce the mesh deformation. Numerical examples demonstrate that intuitive and qualified FE mesh deformations can be obtained with manipulation of the Skeleton-Section template.
Baojun Li, Lei Yang, Ping Hu, Xiuping Liu,
Concurrent Editing of Styling and Structure of Auto Body Based on Metamodels.
Digital Home (ICDH), 2014 5th International Conference on. IEEE, 2014: 331-336.

In this paper, a method is proposed to manipulate a pair of curve networks, one of which is the contour of the styling model of a car and the other the skeleton of the corresponding structural model. The relation between the two curve networks is built. We encode every points in one of the curve network in the local frames of neighboring points in the other curve network. Therefore the encoded curve network can be recovered with respect to the update of the reference one by preserving the relation encoded in the local frames. In order to get a updated curve network as similar as its original shape, we solve the above problem as an optimization with prescribed deformation as hard constraint and curvature preservation as soft. Eventually, the curve network changes at the area user modified and best preserves its original geometry at the rest part. In this way, we can transfer the geometric variation of the styling model to the FE mesh of the vehichle structure providing an initial accessment to the design concept with relatively detailed FE models.
Baojun Li, Lei Yang, Mingzeng Liu, Ping Hu, Bo Wang,
Skeleton-driven Model Editing for Auto-body Structural Analysis.
湖南大学学报: 自然科学版, 2014, 41(8): 7-12.

In this study, we proposed a hybrid deformation method for modifying the general shape of the FE model in structural design of vehicle body. The control volume based deformation is adopted here to deform the FE mesh. In order to perform deformation of the given mesh semantically, a skeleton is pre-defined by the user in which each curve has its explicit engineering content. Nodes in the control volumes that are close to the skeleton are associated to the skeleton. Therefore the positions of these nodes can be updated according to the change of the skeleton. And the positions of the rest of the nodes are solved in a Laplacian-based deformation. Finally with the updated control volumes the FE mesh is mofified.
